# Tone — Portfolio (ashuzaifa)

## Core Feeling

The visitor should feel like they've stumbled onto someone interesting —
not a résumé, not a showcase reel. More like a logbook from someone
mid-expedition, where the discoveries are still happening.

## Voice

Thoughtful and precise. No filler words, no buzzword stacking.
Every line earns its place. When something is technical, it's explained
with clarity — not dumbed down, not gatekept.

Write like someone who has actually thought about what they're saying.

## Personality Traits to Express

- Curious — genuinely, not performatively
- Deliberate — choices are made, not defaulted to
- Understated confidence — the work speaks; the copy doesn't oversell
- A little literary — not academic, not casual, somewhere in between

## Visual Tone (for design decisions)

Inspired by: Late Checkout, Obys Agency, David Heckhoff, Zentry.
The common thread: immersive, intentional, world-building.
Not a template. Every section should feel like it belongs to THIS site.

Bold and creative — but never loud for loudness's sake.
Atmosphere matters. Whitespace is not emptiness, it's breathing room.

## What to Avoid

- Dry, copy-paste portfolio energy ("Hi, I'm X. I'm passionate about Y.")
- Overselling ("innovative", "cutting-edge", "passionate developer")
- Generic section names (Hero, About, Skills, Contact — reframe these)
- Any tone that could belong to anyone else's portfolio

## Writing Examples

### Instead of:

"I am a passionate IoT engineering student with experience in
embedded systems and cloud platforms."

### Write like:

"IoT sits at a rare intersection — not purely hardware, not purely
software. It's the domain where a single person can take an idea
from concept to working device. That's what drew me in."

## Placeholders

- [ ] Decide on section naming convention (e.g. "Field Notes" instead
      of "Projects", "Signal" instead of "Contact")
- [ ] Confirm color palette direction (dark/moody vs warm/editorial)
- [ ] Decide on motion/animation approach (subtle scroll triggers vs
      more cinematic like Zentry)
